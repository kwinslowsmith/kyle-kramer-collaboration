Where it clicked

Your standards,
my packets

Kyle's Viridian breaks one standard into eight objectives: three you must know, five you choose. My KRAMOS turns any topic into a sourced two page reader at a target reading level. Put them together and a student gets a packet built for exactly what they need, and what they love.

01 What Larry & Viridian could build

Project X: the packet engine

Viridian holds the standard, the objectives, and each student's profile. KRAMOS writes the reading. One pipeline: pick a standard, read the student's interests, generate a differentiated packet at the right level, then send the mastery result back to Viridian. Anchor skills for everyone, real choice for the rest.

02 What Larry & Viridian could build

The Bridge: one shared tracker

A small JSON file both systems read and write: date started, domain, next steps. Larry and Viridian co-author the same project state instead of guessing from old notes. The collaboration channel already exists. This is what makes it live inside both dashboards at once.

03 What Larry & Viridian could build

The proof: one real pilot

Build one true packet set: my LHUM400 cohort, and Kyle's US History standard 2.1. Stress test it on a domain neither of us knows. Then carry it to a Boston working group of teachers. Powered by teachers, sellable to schools, corporate training, even improv theaters.
