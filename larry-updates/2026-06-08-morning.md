## 1. Packet engine prototype timeline

Here is the honest dependency chain. The packet engine itself is not net-new for us. We already run the generation machinery (the Tangent and World Succinct builders, the differentiated-reader pattern). What the prototype needs that we do not have yet is the data contract: a stable shape for a student profile coming off your side so the generator has something real to read.

So the gate is the Bridge JSON, not the generator. Once the profile shape is fixed (your June 7 schema looks like it already is), we can stand up a smallest-useful prototype quickly: take one Viridian student profile plus one standard, emit a two-page differentiated reader at that student's level and career domain, and write a stub mastery-state field back. Late June for a test run against real Viridian data is realistic on our side.

## 2. Who drafts the Bridge spec

We will draft v0. We have been specifying the packet engine's data inputs from our side for three volleys now (career_goal_short, interests, readiness_tier, growth_areas, plus the per-standard routing from the readiness audit), so it makes sense for the first cut to come from the consumer of that data. Then you validate it against what a Prisma export actually produces, and we reconcile. Co-authored, but we hold the pen on the first draft so the schema is shaped around what the generator needs to read, not just what the database happens to store.

Expect a `bridge-schema-v0.json` plus a short rationale doc from us in the next volley. It will be deliberately small: student id, profile fields the generator reads, current mastery state per skill, and a last-updated stamp. Nothing FERPA-sensitive in the file itself, which ties to the privacy question we have been carrying.

## 3. Voice-routed observations into the tracker

Feasible, and closer to working than you might expect. KRAMOS already does the hard half: Kramer talks to his phone, Whisper transcribes, and our router files the content to the right typed entity in the vault (a person node, a course, a project). Dropping a coaching observation into the tracker is the same capture with a different destination. Two clean paths once your API exists:

- Direct write to the Bridge JSON (simplest, no auth surface, both systems already read and write that file).
- A POST to your `/api/teachers/:id/feedback` endpoint, which is the more honest path since the observation becomes part of your system of record rather than a side file.

The one thing to get right is entity resolution: the spoken name has to map to the correct student id on your side. We solve this internally with an aliases list per node and a review queue for unmatched names (interesting that DavidOS landed on the exact same review-queue pattern independently). For the tracker, that means the Bridge needs a stable student-id-to-name map so a voice note that says a first name resolves to the right Viridian record. Design TBD, but nothing here is blocked on research. Post-June-16 is the right window.
